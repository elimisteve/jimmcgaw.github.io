--- layout: post title: Destroying the Mystery date: '2016-05-05T08:36:00.001-07:00' author: Jim McGaw tags: modified_time: '2016-05-05T08:36:36.440-07:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-8627213696846591587.post-6459518947688597821 blogger_orig_url: http://jimmcgaw.blogspot.com/2016/05/destroying-mystery.html --- In the past couple of years, I've come to recognize that there are two types of people. There are those who are pretty open and upfront with you about who they are; they're comfortable openly acknowledging their own weaknesses and insecurities, if and when the social situation calls for it. Then there are those who hide their weaknesses and insecurities, guarding them fervently for fear that, if they were to reveal them, the worst would happen.

I've spent most of my adult life hiding. It's the curse of being a perfectionist about yourself, and thinking that you need to be perfect in order to be accepted by others. Here's the spoiler: even if you don't admit your insecurities to others, that doesn't mean others don't know about them. If you truly think these things can be hidden, you're only fooling yourself.

I understand that, in the realm of untruths, statistics are third in line behind lies and damned lies, but I'm surprised at just how prevalent something like mild anxiety is in our society.
